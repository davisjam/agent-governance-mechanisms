Agent Governance Mechanisms

The controls that keep a fleet of autonomous coding agents productive while holding the cost of their failures within bounds — 51 controls across three roles, each written like a design pattern: the recurring failure it kills, and why it is not just the cheaper thing everyone already does.

Distilled from the case study in Cheap Code, Costly Judgment: A Case Study on Governable Agentic Software Engineering. Identities are redacted; the mechanisms are real.

Agent (20)govern the fleet: briefs, dispatch, gates, mediators, lifecycle, governance docs.
Models-bridge (11)the MBSE substrate — typed models agents read & the codebase is generated from. The scaling enabler.
Product (20)govern the shipped artifact: models, validators, tests, provenance, repair vocabulary.
